Yes, I’ve mentored junior and mid-level engineers in several teams.

My approach is a mix of guidance and autonomy:

* I try not to just give answers, but help them understand *how to think* — breaking 
problems down and making trade-offs
* At the same time, I make sure they’re not blocked for too long

What works well for me:

* regular 1:1s to understand their goals and challenges
* pair programming / code reviews as the main teaching tool
* encouraging them to take ownership of small features and grow from there

I also focus a lot on confidence — creating a safe space where it’s okay to ask 
questions and make mistakes.

👉 Overall, I see mentoring as helping someone become independent and confident, 
not dependent on me.
