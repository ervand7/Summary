I start from requirements, not technology — mainly load, latency, consistency, and team
constraints.

Then I make key architectural decisions:

* Monolith vs microservices based on complexity and scaling needs
* Sync vs async depending on latency vs reliability (queues for decoupling)
* Storage choice (SQL for consistency, NoSQL for scale/access patterns)
* API style (REST vs gRPC based on clients and performance needs)

After that, I pick the stack:

* prioritize team expertise and operational simplicity
* choose tools that are proven and easy to maintain, not trendy

Finally, I design for:

* scalability (horizontal scaling, stateless services)
* observability (metrics, logs, tracing)
* failure handling (retries, idempotency, timeouts)

👉 In short: *requirements → trade-offs → simple, scalable, maintainable solution*
