Scaling from 3 to 30 instances makes local caches fragmented and inefficient.

With local caches:
* Same data is cached many times
* Cache hit rate drops with random routing
* Memory and DB load increase

With shared caches (Redis/Memcached):
* All instances reuse the same cache
* Hit rate stays stable
* Scaling does not duplicate cached data

Key insight:
* Horizontal scaling can also multiply cache fragmentation and DB pressure

Interview version:
Scaling many instances fragments local caches and increases downstream load, while
shared caches remain efficient and consistent.
