```text
Protect the system by isolating failures and applying backpressure, so slow dependencies
don’t overload everything else.

Main protections:
* Timeouts — stop waiting too long
* Circuit breakers — stop calling unhealthy services
* Retries with backoff and limits
* Bounded goroutines and worker pools
* Concurrency limits and fail-fast behavior

Why it matters:
* Slow dependencies increase latency
* Higher latency increases concurrency
* Requests pile up and trigger cascading failures

Key insight:
* When downstreams slow down, the system must slow down or shed load

Interview version:
You protect high-load systems with timeouts, circuit breakers, bounded concurrency,
and backpressure to isolate failures and prevent cascading overload.
```
