SAML = login via another trusted system

Instead of checking passwords yourself, you trust someone else to do it

---

### 👉 Simple example

You open a service →
it says:

> “Go login via company account (e.g. Okta)”

Then:

1. You log in to Okta
2. Okta tells your service:

   > “This user is legit”
3. You are logged in

---

### 👉 Key idea

* Your service does NOT check password
* There is a trusted party (IdP) that does
* It sends you a signed XML (proof)

---

### 👉 One-liner

👉 SAML = someone else authenticates the user and sends you a signed proof
