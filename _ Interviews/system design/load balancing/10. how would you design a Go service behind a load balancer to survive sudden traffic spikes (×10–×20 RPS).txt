To survive ×10–×20 traffic spikes, a Go service must shed load early and protect
downstreams.

Key techniques:
* Backpressure and bounded concurrency
* Fail fast with 429/503
* Strict timeouts everywhere
* DB pool limits and circuit breakers
* Graceful degradation and caching
* Readiness checks and slow pod warm-up
* Monitor p95/p99 latency and saturation

Key insight:
* Spikes grow faster than autoscaling can react

Interview version:
I protect the system with bounded concurrency, strict timeouts, downstream
protection, fail-fast behavior, and graceful degradation so traffic spikes
don’t cause cascading failures.
