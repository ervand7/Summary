Load balancers can increase DB load by multiplying concurrency across instances.

Common causes:
* Connection pool explosion
* Cache stampedes and cold caches
* Traffic arriving faster than DB capacity
* Autoscaling creating many cold instances

Key insight:
* The DB sees the combined concurrency of all instances

Interview version:
Load balancers can overload databases by multiplying connection pools, cache misses,
and downstream concurrency across many service instances.
