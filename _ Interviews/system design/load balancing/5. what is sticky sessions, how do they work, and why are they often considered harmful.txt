Sticky sessions mean a client is always routed to the same backend instance.

How it works:
* LB remembers the client → instance mapping
* Usually via cookies or IP hashing

Problems:
* Uneven load distribution
* Worse scaling and fault tolerance
* Encourages stateful services

When useful:
* WebSockets
* Legacy systems

Interview version:
Sticky sessions bind clients to one instance, simplifying state handling but reducing
scalability and resilience.
