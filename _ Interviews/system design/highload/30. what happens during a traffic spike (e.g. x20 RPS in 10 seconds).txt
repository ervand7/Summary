Traffic spikes cause overload before autoscaling can react.

What happens:
* Latency grows
* In-flight requests explode (`RPS × latency`)
* DB pools and memory get exhausted
* Timeouts and retries amplify the load
* Cascading failures begin

With protection:
* Backpressure limits accepted work
* Excess traffic is rejected or queued
* System stays slow but alive

Key insight:
* Systems usually fail from accumulated waiting, not raw traffic

Interview version:
Traffic spikes rapidly increase latency and concurrency, exhausting resources;
backpressure and load shedding keep the system stable instead of collapsing.
