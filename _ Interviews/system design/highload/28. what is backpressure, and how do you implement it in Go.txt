Backpressure prevents a system from accepting more work than it can handle.

Why it matters:
* Downstreams are slower than incoming traffic
* Without limits, requests pile up and exhaust memory

Common Go techniques:
* Worker pools
* Buffered channels
* Semaphores
* DB connection limits
* HTTP 429 / fail-fast

Key insight:
* Backpressure turns overload into controlled failure

Interview version:
Backpressure limits concurrency and incoming work to prevent overload and keep the
system stable under high traffic.
