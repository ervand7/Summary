Correct answer:
- At least once, and potentially multiple times (Kafka provides at-least-once by default).

Explanation:
By default, Kafka guarantees at-least-once delivery semantics: every message
will be delivered and processed at least once, but in case of failures
(consumer crash, rebalance, retries, commit timeouts), the same message may be
processed more than once.

Kafka does not guarantee at-most-once or exactly-once without additional configuration.

To achieve exactly-once semantics, you need:
- Kafka transactions,
- proper handling of consumer offsets,
- idempotency or transactional guarantees on the producer and sink side.

Summary:
- at-most-once → messages may be lost
- at-least-once → duplicates are possible
- exactly-once → possible, but only with explicit configuration and constraints
