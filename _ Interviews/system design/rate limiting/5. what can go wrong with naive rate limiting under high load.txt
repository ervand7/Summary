Common rate limiting pitfalls:

1. Unfairness
* One client consumes all capacity
* Legit users get throttled

2. Latency amplification
* Requests wait instead of failing fast
* Concurrency and memory usage grow

3. Cascading failures
* Dependencies slow down
* Limiter still allows traffic through

4. Central bottleneck
* Redis or shared limiter becomes overloaded
* Limiter becomes the bottleneck

5. Retry storms
* Clients retry aggressively after 429/503
* Load increases further

6. Distributed inconsistencies
* Limits vary across instances
* Scaling changes effective limits

What good designs include:
* Per-user fairness
* Fast rejection
* Concurrency limits
* Observability

Interview version:
Naive rate limiting can cause unfairness, latency amplification, retry storms, and
cascading failures. Good designs fail fast, enforce fairness, and protect downstream
dependencies.
