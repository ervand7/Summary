Rate limiting protects backend systems by preventing overload of downstream
dependencies.

Without rate limiting:
* Traffic spikes are accepted without control
* Latency increases before errors appear
* Concurrency and resource usage grow
* Timeouts and retries amplify load

What can fail:
* DB and Redis connection pools
* External APIs
* Memory and CPU due to queued requests
* Goroutines blocked on I/O

Benefits:
* Excess load is rejected early
* Downstream systems remain stable
* Prevents resource exhaustion
* Reduces risk of cascading failures

Key insight:
* As latency grows, concurrency grows (`RPS × latency`), which can quickly overwhelm a
system.

Interview version:
Rate limiting bounds load and concurrency, protecting downstream systems from resource
exhaustion, latency amplification, and cascading failures.
