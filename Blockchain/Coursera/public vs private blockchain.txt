**Public Blockchain:**
- Open to anyone.
- Decentralized and transparent.
- Participants can join or leave freely.
- High security due to the number of participants.
- Examples: Bitcoin, Ethereum.

**Private Blockchain:**
- Restricted access, usually to a specific organization or group.
- Centralized control with permissions required to join.
- Transactions are private and visible only to authorized participants.
- Faster and more efficient due to fewer participants.
- Examples: Hyperledger, Corda.

Public blockchains prioritize openness and security, while private blockchains focus
on privacy and control.
