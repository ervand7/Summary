Why databases hit limits early:

* Writes require locks, transactions, and coordination
* Databases handle far fewer concurrent connections than app servers
* Each connection is expensive in memory and CPU
* Slow queries increase latency and pile up waiting requests
* All app instances share the same database, increasing contention

Typical symptoms:
* Connection pool exhaustion
* Query queues and lock contention
* System-wide latency spikes

Key insight:
* Databases are usually the hardest part to scale
* High-RPS architectures are designed mainly to protect the database
