# Stage 2 — Embeddings

## 1. What embeddings are

Embeddings are numerical representations of text.

The AI converts text into vectors (arrays of numbers) that represent meaning.

Example:

* “I love cats” → `[0.91, 0.44, 0.77]`
* “Cats are amazing” → `[0.89, 0.46, 0.75]`

Because meanings are similar, vectors are close.

---

## 2. Why text can be converted into vectors

Neural networks learn language patterns during training.

The model learns:

* similar words → nearby vectors
* unrelated words → distant vectors

Example:

* “dog” is close to “puppy”
* “database” is far from “banana”

This allows mathematical comparison of meaning.

---

## 3. Similarity search

Similarity search means:

> “Find text with similar meaning.”

Not exact keyword matching.

Example:

User asks:

> “How can I reset password?”

System may find:

* “Password recovery”
* “Forgot password flow”

even if words are different.

---

## 4. Cosine similarity

Cosine similarity measures how similar two vectors are.

Main idea:

* vectors pointing in similar directions → similar meaning
* vectors pointing differently → different meaning

Range:

* `1` → very similar
* `0` → unrelated
* `-1` → opposite

\cos(\theta)=\frac{A\cdot B}{|A||B|}

You usually use libraries — not manual math.

---

## 5. Chunking documents

Large documents are split into smaller pieces called chunks.

Why:

* embedding models have token limits
* smaller chunks improve search precision

Example:

Large document:

* chunk 1 → lines 1–100
* chunk 2 → lines 101–200

Each chunk gets its own embedding.

---

## 6. Chunk size and overlap

### Chunk size

How much text is inside one chunk.

Example:

* 200 tokens
* 500 tokens
* 1000 tokens

### Overlap

Repeating some text between neighboring chunks.

Example:

* chunk 1 → lines 1–100
* chunk 2 → lines 90–190

Why overlap helps:

* prevents losing context at chunk boundaries

---

## 7. Embedding models

Special AI models that convert text into vectors.

Examples:

* OpenAI embedding models
* BGE models
* E5 models

Goal:

> capture semantic meaning of text.

---

## 8. Storing embeddings

Embeddings are stored in vector databases.

Why:

* efficient similarity search over millions of vectors

Popular solutions:

* Pinecone
* Qdrant
* Weaviate
* pgvector for PostgreSQL

---

## 9. Searching relevant chunks

Flow:

### Step 1

User question:

> “How do I reset my password?”

### Step 2

Question becomes embedding.

### Step 3

System searches nearest vectors.

### Step 4

Most relevant chunks are returned.

### Step 5

Chunks are sent into the LLM.

This is the core of RAG systems.

---

## 10. Re-ranking basics

Initial vector search is fast but approximate.

Re-ranking improves result quality.

Flow:

1. vector DB returns top 20 chunks
2. re-ranker analyzes them more carefully
3. best chunks move higher

Result:

* more accurate context
* better final LLM answers
