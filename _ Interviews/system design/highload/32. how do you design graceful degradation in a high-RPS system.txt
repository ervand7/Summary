Graceful degradation means the system stays available in a limited mode instead
of failing completely.

Typical techniques:
* Keep core functionality alive
* Disable non-critical features
* Use cached or partial responses
* Fail fast on optional dependencies
* Shed excess load early

Under overload:
* Expensive work is skipped or delayed
* Availability is prioritized over completeness

Key insight:
* Users prefer degraded service over timeouts

Interview version:
Graceful degradation keeps the system responsive under overload by prioritizing
critical functionality, using fallbacks, and shedding non-essential work.
