> Django middleware is a chain of hooks that wraps request and response processing 
globally. It is used for cross-cutting concerns like authentication, logging, security 
headers, sessions, and rate limiting.

> A custom middleware is usually designed as a thin, reusable layer that inspects the 
request before the view, optionally short-circuits it, and can also process the 
response after the view. For logging, I would capture method, path, status code, 
latency, and correlation ID. For authentication, I would validate the token/session, 
attach user context to the request, and reject unauthorized requests early.

Senior-level note:

> Middleware should stay lightweight and not contain heavy business logic; complex 
logic should go into dedicated services.

One-liner:

> Middleware is Django’s global request/response interception layer, and I design 
custom middleware for concerns like logging or auth by keeping it thin, fast, and
focused on request context plus early validation.
