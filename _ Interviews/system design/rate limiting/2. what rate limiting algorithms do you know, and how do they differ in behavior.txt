Rate limiting algorithms differ mainly in burst handling and fairness.

1. Fixed window
Pros: Simple, low overhead
Cons: Bursty, unfair at window boundaries


2. Sliding window
Pros: Fair, smooth rate enforcement
Cons: Higher memory and CPU cost


3. Token bucket
Pros: Allows short bursts. Most practical and widely used
Cons: More complex than window counters


4. Leaky bucket
Pros: Smooth, predictable traffic
Cons: Adds latency. Bursts may be delayed
